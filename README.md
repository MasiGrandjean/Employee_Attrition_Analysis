
# HR Analytics - Employee Attrition Analysis

This project analyzes employee attrition using HR data to identify the main reasons why employees leave a company and provide data-driven retention recommendations.

## Project Objective

The goal of this analysis is to answer key business questions such as:

- Why are employees leaving the company?
- Which departments have the highest turnover?
- Does salary impact attrition?
- Does workload increase resignation risk?
- How does employee satisfaction affect retention?

---

## Dataset Information

- Source: HR Analytics Dataset
- Total Records: 14,999
- Features: 10 columns

### Main Columns

- satisfaction_level
- last_evaluation
- number_project
- average_monthly_hours
- time_spend_company
- work_accident
- left
- promotion_last_5years
- Department
- salary

## Key Insights

- Employees with lower satisfaction are more likely to leave.
- Low salary employees show the highest attrition rate.
- High workload may increase resignation risk.
- Some departments require stronger retention strategies.
- Promotions may positively impact retention.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Improve employee satisfaction programs
- Review compensation for low salary groups
- Monitor workload balance
- Create internal promotion opportunities
- Focus retention efforts on high-risk departments
